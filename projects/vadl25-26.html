VADL 25–26 · Vehicle & Payload Design

Vanderbilt Aerospace Design Lab (Rocket Team) · Mechanical Design, Integration & Testing

The team will launch a full-scale vehicle and, using an apogee control system, achieve a controlled apogee of 4,500 ft AGL. The rocket will recover via dual-deployment—drogue at apogee and main at ≥ 500 ft AGL—so all sections land safely and are ready to re-launch without repair. After touchdown, the tail-mounted HAUS payload will autonomously acquire ≥ 50 mL of soil with a rotating shovel and analyze the retained sample for required metrics (e.g., nitrate-nitrogen, pH, electrical conductivity) within 15 minutes, with results recorded for post-flight reporting.
