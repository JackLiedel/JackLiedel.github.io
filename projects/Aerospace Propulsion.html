Aerospace Design – Numerical Modeling & Propulsion

Student · Aerospace Design Course · Vanderbilt University · 2024–2025

This course sequence focused on building numerical tools for aerospace systems: modeling rocket ascent and recovery, interplanetary transfers, nozzle performance, and ramjet propulsion. Each assignment combined governing equations, MATLAB implementations, and engineering design trade studies.

Below are selected assignments from the class, each presented as a short project with its own slide deck and supporting analysis.

Assignment #1

Starhsip Analysis

This project analyzes SpaceX’s evolution and engineering philosophy. Later, a full numerical simulation of a Starship Earth-to-Mars trajectory to evaluate the feasibility of its interplanetary mission architecture is developed.

Assignment #2

Post-Apogee Recovery & Parachute Deployment

Modeled post-apogee flight of a high-power rocket, including main-parachute inflation, shock-cord loads, and landing impact. Selected an optimal deployment altitude that satisfied both load limits and recovery constraints.

View slides (PDF)
Assignment #3

Pre-Apogee Ascent with Wind & Launch Angle

Built a four-phase ascent model to simulate rocket motion from ignition through burnout, including windcocking, side-wind profiles, and non-linear density variation. Compared trajectories for different wind speeds and launch angles.

View slides (PDF)
Assignment #4

Impulsive Earth-to-Mars Transfer Δv Family

Modeled a three-body Earth–spacecraft–Mars problem with impulsive burns from LEO to LMO. Generated a family of Δv solutions that reach Mars while maintaining safe atmospheric entry conditions.

View slides (PDF)
Assignment #5

Low-Thrust Earth-to-Mars Mission Design

Extended the transfer analysis to include constant and solar-powered low thrust. Compared travel time, mass evolution, and Mars-arrival conditions for different thrust profiles and thrust-reversal strategies.

View slides (PDF)
Assignment #6

Nozzle Area–Mach Relations & Thrust Coefficient

Implemented a converging–diverging nozzle solver to relate exit Mach number to area ratio and computed thrust coefficient versus ambient pressure for a high-expansion nozzle.

View slides (PDF)
Assignment #7

Ideal Ramjet Ignition & Specific Thrust

Determined the earliest flight Mach number at which an ideal ramjet can ignite without shocks at the nozzle exit, and analyzed specific thrust as a function of flight Mach number and area ratio.

View slides (PDF)
Assignment #8

Real-Gas Ramjet Performance

Built on the ideal-ramjet work by incorporating real-gas properties. Selected an optimal area ratio and recomputed specific thrust using temperature-dependent cp and γ to quantify the performance penalty relative to the ideal model.
